## 📊 Insights

- A small number of customers contribute the majority of total revenue, indicating a high-value customer segment driving business performance.

- The Electronics category generates the highest revenue, suggesting strong demand for higher-priced tech products compared to other categories.

- Lower-priced categories (such as Grocery or Stationery) contribute less to total revenue despite potentially higher unit sales.

- Employee salaries vary significantly within departments, with certain individuals earning noticeably above their department average.

- Departments show uneven salary distributions, indicating possible differences in experience levels, roles, or performance-based pay.

- Cities are not evenly distributed in terms of high-value customers; Tampa has the highest number of Gold loyalty customers.

- The concentration of Gold customers in a single city suggests geographic clustering of high-value customers.

- Loyalty tiers appear segmented across cities, with many cities having mostly Silver or Bronze customers and fewer Gold-level customers.

- Revenue and customer loyalty patterns together suggest that both product category and customer location play important roles in overall business performance.
